Setting up approval flows for multi-entity businesses with mixed currency balances

Approval flows route payments to the right reviewers before money moves. This guide covers multi-entity setups and mixed-currency balances — the trickiest configuration.

Before you start

You'll need admin access and a list of entities, balances, and the people who should approve payments at each level. Gather this first — it makes the flow builder quick.

Note. Approval flows apply to payments initiated after the flow is activated. In-flight payments keep the rules they were created under.

1. Open the approvals builder

From the dashboard, open Settings, then Approvals. Click New flow. Give the flow a clear name — we recommend entity + currency, e.g. "Equals UK · GBP".

[ product shot · approvals builder — new flow dialog ]

2. Define thresholds

Thresholds set which payments need extra review. A common pattern for a mixed-currency setup:

  • Under £1,000 (or currency equivalent) — no approval needed
  • £1,000–£10,000 — one approver from Finance
  • Over £10,000 — two approvers, at least one Director

Thresholds convert to each balance's currency using the mid-market rate at the time of initiation.

3. Assign approvers by entity

Under each threshold, pick the people or groups who can approve. You can mix named users and roles (e.g. "any Director"). For multi-entity setups, assign per-entity approvers so that UK payments go to UK directors and EU payments go to EU directors.

[ product shot · assigning approvers by entity ]

4. Test and activate

  1. Click Test flow and run a dry-run payment.
  2. Confirm the right approvers receive the review request.
  3. Activate the flow. Existing payments in progress are unaffected.
