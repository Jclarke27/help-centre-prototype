How to create a card and link it to a balance

A complete walkthrough — issue a virtual or physical card, link it to a balance, set limits, and hand it off to a cardholder. Halfway through, we cover how to freeze or cancel a card you've already issued.

Before you start

You'll need admin access to issue cards in Equals Money. Take a moment to decide:

  • Who will hold the card
  • Whether it should be virtual (instant) or physical (3–5 working days)
  • Which balance the card should draw from
  • Initial daily, weekly, and monthly limits

Gathering this up front makes the form quick — most teams can issue a card in under two minutes once they know the answers.

Tip. Cards inherit currency from the linked balance. If you link a card to your USD balance, it spends in USD without converting through GBP.

1. Choose virtual or physical

Virtual cards are issued instantly. They're ideal for online subscriptions, advertising spend, or one-off purchases. The full card number, CVV, and expiry are revealed once when the card is created — store them in your password manager.

Physical cards are mailed to the cardholder's address and arrive in 3–5 working days. They support contactless, chip-and-PIN, ATM withdrawals, and Apple Pay or Google Pay.

Many teams issue a virtual card first for immediate use, and order a physical card in parallel for in-person spend.

2. Pick a balance to link

The card spends from the balance you link. You have a few options:

  • Link to a single-currency balance (e.g. EUR) — the card transacts in that currency with no FX conversion.
  • Link to your home-currency balance (e.g. GBP) — most flexible, but international spend uses live mid-market FX rates.
  • Use a sub-balance if your team has separate cost centres or projects you want to track independently.

You can change the linked balance later by editing the card. Any pending authorisations stay against the original balance until they settle.

3. Set initial limits

Three caps apply to every card:

  • Daily — resets at midnight UTC.
  • Weekly — resets each Monday at 00:00 UTC.
  • Monthly — resets on the 1st at 00:00 UTC.

Set them a little tighter than you think — limits are easy to raise later, but a runaway charge is harder to recover. As a rule of thumb, start at roughly twice the cardholder's typical weekly spend.

You can also restrict the card to specific merchant categories. Block gambling, adult content, or travel categories on cards that only need to pay for SaaS or office supplies.

Freeze or cancel a card from the dashboard

Step-by-step: find the card in your Cards page, open it, and choose Freeze. The card is frozen instantly — new transactions are blocked, but in-flight authorisations complete normally.

To cancel a card, open the card and choose Cancel. Cancellation is permanent — the card number is dead and you'll need to issue a fresh one. From the same screen you can issue a replacement, which carries over the linked balance and limits but generates a new card number, CVV, and expiry.

Freeze is reversible. Cancel is not. If you're not sure whether to terminate a card permanently — start with freeze. Unfreeze the same way you froze it.

4. Hand the card off

Once the card is created, the cardholder gets an email with an activation link. From there they:

  • Set their own PIN (for physical cards).
  • Add the card to Apple Pay or Google Pay if they want mobile payments.
  • See their card details, transaction history, and current spend against limits in the app.

Cardholders can freeze and unfreeze their own card, but they can't change limits or cancel it — those controls stay with admins.

5. Track activity and update later

Every transaction lands on the card's detail page in real time. From there you can:

  • Send notifications to a Slack channel or email distribution list when spend events happen.
  • Export per-card statements as CSV or PDF for end-of-month reconciliation.
  • Adjust limits, merchant categories, or the linked balance as the cardholder's needs change.
  • Bulk-edit cards if you're rolling out a change across a team — limit increases for a season, restricted categories for a new policy.

For ongoing reconciliation, connect Xero, QuickBooks, or NetSuite from Settings → Integrations and we'll sync transactions nightly.

Frequently asked questions

What's the difference between freeze and cancel?
Freeze is reversible — unfreeze the same way and the card resumes. Cancel is permanent — the card number is dead and you have to issue a replacement.

Can a cardholder issue their own card?
No — only admins can create cards. Cardholders can manage the card they hold (view details, freeze, see transactions) but can't issue new ones.

Are virtual cards safe for online subscriptions?
Yes, and we recommend them. If a merchant is breached, you can freeze the card instantly from the dashboard without disrupting other subscriptions or in-person spend.

What happens to pending payments if I cancel a card?
In-flight authorisations complete; any new attempts are declined. Refunds for the cancelled card land back in the linked balance.
